## Solution: HiveMap

**Tagline:** *See what the desert hides. Grow what the land remembers.*

### What It Is

**HiveMap** is a hybrid technology-and-services platform that uses **drone-based environmental sensing** fused with **traditional ecological knowledge (TEK)** to identify, assess, and activate abandoned sites across the Sahel for smallholder agroforestry rehabilitation.

It is not just a mapping tool. It is an **end-to-end site activation pipeline** — from aerial survey to cooperative business plan — that turns invisible micro-assets (abandoned structures with hidden water, wild pollinators, remnant crops) into productive, climate-resilient agricultural hubs.

### Provider

**HiveMap SAS** — a for-profit social enterprise incorporated in Nouakchott, Mauritania, with a technical operations hub in Dakar, Senegal. Structured as a **B-Corp equivalent** under Mauritanian commercial law, with a dual mandate: financial sustainability and measurable impact on smallholder livelihoods.

**Founding team profile:**
- Drone/remote sensing engineer (technical lead)
- Agronomist with Sahel field experience
- Community development specialist with cooperative formation expertise
- Traditional beekeeper / TEK advisor (Adrar region)

### How It Works

#### Phase 1: Aerial Discovery
- Deploy survey drones equipped with **thermal, multispectral, and LiDAR sensors** over target zones (20 km radius around partner cooperatives)
- Automated detection of: abandoned structures, subsurface moisture signatures, vegetation anomalies, pollinator flight patterns (thermal)
- Output: **georeferenced site inventory** with raw sensor data

#### Phase 2: TEK Validation
- Local knowledge holders (beekeepers, herders, elders) review drone findings via a **tablet-based interface** with satellite imagery overlays
- They annotate sites with traditional knowledge: historical water sources, seasonal bee migration routes, soil quality indicators, structural integrity assessments
- Output: **enriched site profiles** combining sensor data and indigenous knowledge

#### Phase 3: Site Scoring & Prioritisation
- Proprietary **HiveMap Score** (0–100) ranking each site on:
  - Water availability (30%)
  - Pollinator presence and diversity (20%)
  - Structural reusability (15%)
  - Soil rehabilitation potential (15%)
  - Proximity to cooperative members (10%)
  - Land tenure clarity (10%)
- Output: **ranked portfolio of activation-ready sites**

#### Phase 4: Activation Planning
- For top-scoring sites, generate a **site activation plan** including:
  - Recommended crops (grape varieties, acacia, date palm, moringa)
  - Apiary placement and colony management plan
  - Structural rehabilitation scope and cost estimate
  - Water harvesting / micro-irrigation design
  - 3-year financial projection for the cooperative
- Output: **bankable project proposal** suitable for submission to development funders (GIZ, USAID, AFD, World Bank Climate Investment Funds)

#### Phase 5: Ongoing Monitoring
- Quarterly drone re-surveys to track vegetation growth, hive health, water levels
- Dashboard for cooperatives showing yield projections, actual vs. planned
- Impact data packaged for funders and carbon credit verification

### What Makes It Different

1. **TEK integration is not optional — it is core.** Other agri-drone companies treat local knowledge as anecdotal. HiveMap treats it as structured data with equal weight to sensor readings.
2. **End-to-end pipeline.** Competitors stop at mapping. HiveMap continues through to bankable project proposals that cooperatives can submit to funders.
3. **Pollinator-first approach.** Wild bee colonies are used as biological indicators of site viability — a methodology validated in ecological research but never operationalised commercially.
4. **Built for the Sahel, not adapted from temperate models.** Hardware, software, and workflows designed for extreme heat, dust, low connectivity, and communal land tenure systems.

### Key Assumptions & Risks

| Assumption | Risk if Wrong | Mitigation |
|------------|--------------|------------|
| Abandoned structures create measurable microclimates | Drone sensors may not detect at sufficient resolution | Pilot with known sites; validate with ground-truth sensors |
| TEK holders will participate | Distrust of technology or extractive research | Co-design compensation; cooperative ownership of data |
| Development funders will accept HiveMap scores | Novel methodology lacks peer-reviewed validation | Partner with university for independent validation study |
| Land tenure can be resolved | Legal disputes block rehabilitation | Work only with sites where community claim is uncontested |
| Grape and bee productivity viable in target zones | Climate too extreme for commercial yields | Start with most promising Adrar sites; diversify crop portfolio |
