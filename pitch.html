The Problem

The best land is hidden in plain sight.

🛰️

Resolution Gap

Standard satellites (10-30m resolution) cannot detect building-scale microclimates, underground springs, or pollinator colonies. Most viable micro-sites go undetected.

🧓

Knowledge Dying

The majority of traditional ecological knowledge holders are elderly. Their expertise has no digital interface. When they go, it goes.

💰

Funding Misfire

Climate adaptation spending targets mega-projects. Zero investment in micro-scale, structure-based rehabilitation that delivers results 10x faster.

Lessons Learned

What this journey taught us.

  1. 01 The most powerful sensor isn't on a drone — it's in the memory of a beekeeper who has read the desert for decades. Technology without indigenous knowledge is just expensive noise.
  2. 02 Abandoned doesn't mean empty. Colonial ruins, forgotten wells, and "haunted" buildings are ecological time capsules that preserved microclimates while the surrounding land degraded.
  3. 03 AI-generated market sizing sounds authoritative but demands verification. Every statistic in an AI pipeline must be treated as a hypothesis until independently confirmed.
  4. 04 The Sahel doesn't need more pilot projects — it needs pilots that come with a business model attached. Impact without sustainability is just expensive tourism.
  5. 05 Building for extreme environments forces better design. Offline-first, heat-resistant hardware, multilingual interfaces — constraints that make the product stronger everywhere.
  6. 06 Carbon credits are a revenue stream, not a mission. The moment carbon math drives decisions instead of farmer outcomes, the model fails ethically and commercially.
  7. 07 From five random parameters to a full venture concept in hours — AI radically compresses ideation, but judgement, critique, and domain knowledge remain irreplaceably human.
