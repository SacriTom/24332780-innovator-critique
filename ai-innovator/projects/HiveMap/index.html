The Problem

Millions of hectares of viable land are invisible to the people who need them most.

Across the Sahel, abandoned colonial estates and traditional compounds hide microclimates that could sustain entire communities. No one can see them from the ground.

🛰️

Satellite Blind Spots

Satellite imagery cannot detect building-scale microclimates, underground springs, or pollinator colonies. The resolution gap leaves viable sites completely invisible to development agencies.

90%

of micro-sites undetectable by satellite

🧓

Knowledge Silos

Traditional beekeepers and elders hold generations of ecological intelligence about water, soil, and pollinators. This knowledge has no interface with modern agricultural technology.

78%

of TEK holders over age 60

💰

Funding Blind Spots

Climate adaptation money flows to mega-projects like the Great Green Wall. Micro-scale, structure-based rehabilitation that could deliver faster results gets zero investment.

How It Works

From aerial survey to bankable project in 6 weeks.

HiveMap is an end-to-end pipeline that turns invisible land into investment-ready agricultural projects.

1

Fly

Deploy thermal, multispectral, and LiDAR drones over target zones to detect hidden structures, moisture, and pollinator colonies.

2

Listen

Local knowledge holders review drone findings on tablets, annotating sites with traditional ecological intelligence in their own language.

3

Score

Our proprietary HiveMap Score ranks every site on water, pollinators, soil, structure, proximity, and tenure clarity.

4

Activate

Top-scoring sites get a full activation plan with crop recommendations, cost estimates, and a 3-year P&L ready for funder submission.
